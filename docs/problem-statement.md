# Problem Statement

Many organizations still rely on manual registers, spreadsheets, or disconnected tools to manage their inventory. These traditional methods often lead to inaccurate stock records, delayed updates, poor tracking of product movement, and lack of real-time visibility into warehouse operations. As the volume of products and transactions increases, managing inventory efficiently becomes more challenging and error-prone.

The objective of this project is to design and develop **CoreInventory**, a modular Inventory Management System (IMS) that digitizes and streamlines stock-related operations within a business. The system aims to provide a centralized platform where inventory managers and warehouse staff can easily track, update, and manage product inventory across different locations.

The application should allow users to create and manage product records, monitor stock availability, record incoming goods from suppliers, process outgoing deliveries to customers, transfer stock between warehouse locations, and adjust inventory when discrepancies occur between recorded and physical counts.

In addition, the system should provide a dashboard that offers a real-time overview of inventory operations, including total products in stock, low-stock alerts, pending receipts, and delivery orders. By replacing manual inventory tracking with a digital system, businesses can improve operational efficiency, reduce errors, and maintain accurate inventory records.
